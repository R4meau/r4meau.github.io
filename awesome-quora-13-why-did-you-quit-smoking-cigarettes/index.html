Awesome Quora #13: Why did you quit smoking cigarettes?

Written on
12-18-2019
awesome-quora, quora, quora-answers

Preamble

I read Quora questions/answers regularly.

So in the spirit of redistributing and having an easier access to my favorite stories over there, I decided to publish them on a daily basis here.

Today’s pick is a little different though.

The following question and answer were actually not in my bookmarks. I added them a couple of minutes ago.

I usually search through Google directly and then end up on Quora but I knew if I looked up my search query below, Quora wouldn’t even be among the first 10 results I get.

My search query was “How to properly quit cigarettes”.

Yeah, I’ve been addicted to cigarettes since a couple of years ago. Over 3 years now to be more precise.

And no, this is not the first time I tried quitting.

Oh, and yes, I know how bad it is for my health. I’m aware of my addiction, I feel bad about it every time I finish one.

I forgive myself though.

Because today, I finally found the cure!

Okay, let’s not get too excited here. I finally found the cure for myself, it MIGHT work for somebody else passing by here, so I’m publishing this.

Today I went to the groceries and I decided to not take a taxi to it. I passed by this dude selling lots of stuffs (I might add a picture here later) on the streets and guess what’s included?

Cigarettes of course.

For some, God knows what, reason, I decided to buy 3 of them. Not ONE, but three.

Then I continued my way to the grocery store and got what I needed. I then decided to toke on one while I wait for someone.

I was smoking right in front of the store and I was facing a few cars in the parking lot.

A few minutes later, the window of one of the cars got pulled down and this man called me telling me how bad this is and how my health is important and how he’s married for 40 years now (crazy!)… you know, your typical non smoker.

He managed to be persuasive enough and I ended up throwing away the other two cigarettes.

This dude woke up the Stoic in me. Haha, I swear.

He told me something like:

“Remember you will die, you don’t wanna die suffering my friend.”

Boom! That hit the crap out of me and I thanked him very much for this.

Thanks to this guy, I’m even more aware of my desires and that made me do the research above (again) today and found the answer that fits me the best.

I am a non smoker now and here’s the solution that’s working best for me at the moment.

Why did you quit smoking cigarettes?

The following answer is by Marie Hooke to this particular question.

Here’s how I quit smoking. I started smoking when I was in my early 20’s (I am now 63). Started only smoking occasionally but over time I was up to three packs a day. Mind you I smoked ultra light brands and often times they sat burning up in an ashtray, but nonetheless.

When the cost of cigs went up, I decided it was time to quit. I tried every possible way to stop. You name it…I tried it. I even came up with some ways of my own to try and quit. Nothing worked and if anything each time I failed I would smoke even more out of failure, frustration and guilt.

So…cutting to the chase here….a health issue landed me in the hospital for 10 days which of course I was not able to smoke…it was brutal. While there I was told I couldn’t have a patch because of treatments and my condition. All I could think of was having a smoke. I even went so far as to try and figure out what nurse that was on my case smoked thinking maybe I could play on their sympathies and have them take me outside for just one cigarette.

They sent a woman in to see me and to talk about smoking. This lady looked to be that of a person who may have been a nun or preacher. Without being prejudiced I thought to myself - no way this nice lady could possibly understand what withdrawal is like. Turns out I was very very wrong.

The conversation went like this —- She said…Do you have friends who smoke? I said - well, not really most do not smoke. She asked me how do I feel about that. I went on to explain how I have to go outside to smoke, away from them when we are together. They comment on the smell, making me self-conscious, can’t smoke in their car, they would not ride in mine because of the smell of smoke. Even though I thought I covered up the smoke smell I was only fooling myself.

So then she said - so now you are a non-smoker. WHAT? — was my reaction. She repeated - NOW YOU ARE A NON SMOKER JUST LIKE THEM. Again WHAT? She was right. I had not had a cigarette in 10 days so I was a non smoker…just like the other people I know who do not smoke. I know that sounds crazy and you might think how can it possibly be that simple. Well, it really is that simple. After trying every possible way to quit and failed..all I had to do was change my thinking. I associated myself as a non-smoker, something I so much wanted to be and voila - there I was - a non smoker.

Stop telling yourself you - want to, should, can’t, failed, or you will quit some day or you are trying to quit…stop that talk. It’s a change of mind that will help you quit. It is a decision. Do not make it harder than it has to be. You just become a non-smoker and think like a non-smoker…BE a non-smoker.

If I can do it…anyone can. I loved my smokes and they were with me at all time. It’s been 10 years now and I haven’t even had the slightest desire to smoke because…I am now a non-smoker. You can be too. It’s that easy.

The above answer is by Marie Hooke to this particular question.

Conclusion

I hope you enjoyed this answer as much as I did.

I really recommend you check out other answers to this question on Quora. I may have read it a long time ago and some new and even better answers may have popped up since.
